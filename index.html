Air Traffic Controller Career

Your Guide to a Rewarding Career in Air Traffic Control

1. Eligibility & Foundations

Basic Requirements

  • Be a U.S. citizen
  • Pass medical and security clearances
  • Complete an FAA-approved education program or gain work experience
  • Pass the Air Traffic Skills Assessment (ATSA) exam

Minimum Requirements

  • U.S. Citizen
  • Under age 31 (at Academy start)
  • 3 years of work experience or a Bachelor's degree
  • Speak clear English

The Profile

  • Must pass medical, psychological, and drug screenings
  • High washout rate (up to 50% in training)
  • Demands precision and quick decision-making
  • Mandatory retirement at age 56

2. The 5-Step Hiring Pipeline

1

Application (USAJobs)

Apply via USAJobs during open "off-the-street" bids. Requires resume, transcripts (if applicable), and CTI letter (if CTI graduate).

2

ATSA Exam

Computer-based test assessing skills needed for ATC work. Covers angles/spatial relationships, memory, and decision-making under pressure.

3

Medical, Psychological, & Security

Rigorous checks including vision (20/20 correctable), hearing, drug screening, and a psychological evaluation (MMPI-2).

4

FAA Academy (Oklahoma City)

3-5 months of intensive training. Students receive a salary during training. High failure rate (30-50%).

5

Facility Assignment & OJT

1-3 years of on-the-job training at an assigned facility. Must certify on all positions at the facility.

3. Compensation and Job Outlook

Salary Range

  • Starting: $47,000 (Academy)
  • Developmental: $40,000 - $80,000
  • Certified (CPC): $120,000 - $200,000+
  • Overtime: Time-and-a-half after 8 hours

Benefits

  • Federal employee benefits (TSP, FERS pension)
  • Health, dental, and vision insurance
  • Paid time off and sick leave
  • Union representation (NATCA)

Job Outlook

The FAA hires in cycles, typically every 1-2 years. The next major hiring wave is expected in 2024-2025 due to retirements.

Note: The FAA typically receives over 30,000 applications for 1,000-2,000 positions per hiring bid.

4. ATSA Test Structure and Prep

Test Components

1. Cognitive Ability

  • Math and reading comprehension
  • Logical reasoning
  • Spatial awareness

2. Personality Assessment

  • Work style preferences
  • Stress tolerance
  • Decision-making approach

Prep Tips

  • Start early: Begin preparing 2-3 months before the test date
  • Practice tests: Use official ATSA prep materials
  • Time management: The test is timed - practice working quickly and accurately
  • Focus on weaknesses: Identify and improve in areas where you score lower

Prep Tips from Successful Candidates

Top Study Strategies

1. Start Early

Begin preparing at least 2-3 months before the test date. Consistent, focused study sessions are more effective than cramming.

2. Practice Tests

Take full-length, timed practice tests to simulate the actual testing environment and improve time management.

3. Focus on Weak Areas
